2. **Design the Coil Geometry:**
   - Sketch the coil shape (circular, rectangular) on paper.
   - Use SolidWorks to create a 3D model of the coil using helix or spiral tools.

3. **Set Up SolidWorks Electrical:**
   - Install the SolidWorks Electrical module.
   - Create a new electrical schematic and add components (resistors, capacitors, inductors, connectors).

4. **Model the Coil and Circuit:**
   - Represent the MRI coil as an inductor symbol in the schematic.
   - Add capacitors to form an LC circuit for tuning to the resonant frequency.

5. **Incorporate Connectors and Wiring:**
   - Add appropriate connectors for interfacing with the MRI machine.
   - Connect components with named wires for clarity.

6. **Generate Layout and Simulate:**
   - Use SolidWorks to generate a PCB layout if necessary.
   - Simulate the circuit to verify resonance at the correct frequency, possibly using external tools like SPICE.

7. **Document the Design:**
   - Generate a Bill of Materials and wiring diagram from SolidWorks.

8. **Prototype and Test:**
   - Build the coil and circuit based on the schematic.
   - Measure inductance and capacitance to confirm resonant frequency.

9. **Iterate and Refine:**
   - Adjust the design as needed based on testing results.
   - Modify the SolidWorks model and re-simulate until performance is achieved.

10. **Finalize and Manufacture:**
    - Prepare final schematics, PCB layouts, and assembly instructions.

By following these steps, you can systematically design and document an MRI coil using SolidWorks, ensuring functionality and precision.
