## 2. Physics Equations & Mathematical Models

### 2.1 MR Pulse Sequence Physics

**A. Spin Echo (SE) Signal Intensity**
The signal $S$ for a standard Spin Echo sequence is governed by T1 and T2 relaxation times:
$$ S \propto \rho (1 - e^{-TR/T1}) e^{-TE/T2} $$
*   $TR$: Repetition Time
*   $TE$: Echo Time
*   $\rho$: Proton Density

**B. Gradient Recalled Echo (GRE)**
For GRE sequences with flip angle $\alpha$:
$$ S \propto \rho \sin(\alpha) \frac{1 - e^{-TR/T1}}{1 - \cos(\alpha) e^{-TR/T1}} e^{-TE/T2^*} $$
*   **Ernst Angle** (Optimal $\alpha$): $\alpha_E = \arccos(e^{-TR/T1})$

**C. Adaptive FLAIR (Fluid Attenuated Inversion Recovery)**
Signal nulling for CSF (where $T1_{CSF} \approx 4000ms$):
$$ S(TI) = \rho (1 - 2e^{-TI/T1} + e^{-TR/T1}) $$
Null condition: $TI_{null} = T1 \ln(2)$

### 2.2 RF Coil Design Physics

**A. Biot-Savart Law (Magnetic Field Generation)**
The B-field $B(\mathbf{r})$ generated by current $I$ in wire path $C$:
$$ \mathbf{B}(\mathbf{r}) = \frac{\mu_0 I}{4\pi} \int_C \frac{d\mathbf{l} \times \mathbf{r}'}{|\mathbf{r}'|^3} $$

**B. Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR)**
For an RF coil, SNR corresponds to the $B_1^-$ field sensitivity and resistance $R$:
$$ SNR \propto \frac{\omega_0 B_1^-}{\sqrt{4kT\Delta f R_{eff}}} $$
*   $R_{eff} = R_{coil} + R_{sample}$ (Sample noise dominates at high field)
*   **50-Turn Advantage**: Increases $B_1^-$ density significantly, retaining sample dominance.

**C. Inductance of Micro-Solenoid (50-Turn Element)**
$$ L \approx \mu_0 N^2 \frac{A}{l} $$
*   $N=50$: Inductance scales with $N^2$, requiring high-Q capacitors for distinct resonance.

### 2.3 Advanced Reconstruction Algorithms

**A. Parallel Imaging (SENSE/GRAPPA)**
Reconstruction involves inverting the encoding matrix $E$:
$$ \mathbf{k} = E \mathbf{\rho} + \mathbf{n} \implies \hat{\mathbf{\rho}} = (E^H \Psi^{-1} E)^{-1} E^H \Psi^{-1} \mathbf{k} $$
*   $\Psi$: Noise covariance matrix

**B. Statistical LLM Optimization (StatLLM)**
Bayesian inference where the prior $P(x)$ is learned via LLM statistics:
$$ \hat{x} = \arg\max_x [\log P(y|x) + \lambda \log P_{LLM}(x)] $$
*   Likelihood: $\log P(y|x) = -\|Ax - y\|_2^2$
*   Prior: $\log P_{LLM}(x)$ derived from transformer attention maps.

**C. Quantum Machine Learning (QML) Hamiltonian**
The reconstruction minimizes a cost function encoded in a quantum Hamiltonian $H$:
$$ H = \sum_i h_i \sigma_i^z + \sum_{ij} J_{ij} \sigma_i^z \sigma_j^z $$
*   Optimization finds the ground state $|\psi_g\rangle$ minimizing energy $E = \langle \psi | H | \psi \rangle$.

**D. Geodesic Diffeomorphic Mapping (LDDMM)**
Minimizes energy of velocity field $v_t$:
$$ E(v) = \int_0^1 \|v_t\|_V^2 dt + \frac{1}{\sigma^2} \|I \circ \phi_1^{-1} - J\|^2 $$
*   $\phi_1$: Diffeomorphism at time $t=1$.
